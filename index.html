Who Am I?

Hi, I'm Duo Peng, a biologist with training in genetics (B.S.), bioinformatics (M.S.) and molecular parasitology (Ph.D.). Currently, I am training in the vector biology field as a postdoc, studying the reproductive biology of mosquitoes and how it affects their capacity to transmit malaria.

I'm developing machine learning methods for integrating biological multi-omics dataset to decipher key factors affecting parasite development in the malaria mosquito, which is one of the deadliest animal on earth (for the disease they carry)
When I'm not coding, I am at the lab bench, purifying a male-specific mosquito hormone important for reproduction and study how it is synthesized.

My research interests:
  1. Computational biology, with a focus in omic dataset analysis/mining, and development of computational prediction pipelines.
  2. Mosquito reproductive biology and malaria.
  3. Chagas disease, antigenic variation and CRISPR-based genetic screen in Trypanosoma cruzi.
